# LooksMaxxing Review Checklist

Updated: 2026-03-20 America/Los_Angeles

## Goal

Use this checklist before showing LooksMaxxing to anyone or deciding on the next major build step.

---

## 1) Proof Capture Sequence

Capture screenshots in this order:

1. Hook screen
   - hero orb visible
   - strong headline/subhead
2. Upload screen
   - real image selected
   - input mode visible
3. Scan screen
   - scan progress visible
4. Result screen
   - optimization score
   - tier
   - archetype
   - progression copy
5. Breakdown screen
   - category cards clearly readable
6. Simulation screen
   - now vs after comparison
7. Glow-up tracker screen
   - streak
   - best score
   - timeline graph
   - best-version card
8. Paywall screen
   - free / pro / subscription framing visible
9. Improvement plan screen
   - recommendations with impact / difficulty / time
10. Share screen
   - export-style share card
   - caption tone selector
11. Battle mode screen
   - both sides populated
   - winner declared

If possible, also record one short walkthrough:
- Hook → Upload → Scan → Result → Breakdown → Simulation → Tracker → Share → Battle

---

## 2) Show-Ready Review Questions

### Product feel
- Does LooksMaxxing feel like a real product, not a toy?
- Does it feel like optimization/progression instead of random face judgment?
- Is the emotional hook immediate in the first 10 seconds?

### Clarity
- Can a new person understand what the app does without explanation?
- Are score, tier, archetype, and potential all immediately understandable?
- Does each screen have one clear primary action?

### Visual quality
- Does anything look cramped?
- Does any card feel too text-heavy?
- Are button labels clear and consistent?
- Are any screens visually weaker than the others?

### Retention
- Does the history/tracker screen make you want to come back?
- Does progression to next tier feel motivating?
- Does the uncertainty loop encourage a rerate without sounding fake?

### Virality
- Would someone actually share the share-card screen?
- Do the generated captions feel postable?
- Does battle mode feel fun enough to try with a friend?

### Credibility
- Does the image-derived scoring feel more believable than the earlier prototype?
- Does the battle result feel grounded enough when a second image is uploaded?
- Are there any moments where the app still obviously feels mocked or staged?

---

## 3) Highest-Risk Weak Spots To Watch

1. Battle mode may still need stronger dual-photo presentation polish
2. Timeline graph is functional, but still prototype-grade visually
3. Score engine is image-derived now, but not yet true ML/computer vision
4. Monetization is still framing, not real infrastructure

---

## 4) Decision After Review

After screenshots + walkthrough, decide which branch to take next:

### Branch A — Strong prototype / demo route
Choose this if the concept is strong and the goal is testing reaction.

Next steps:
- keep polishing presentation
- collect feedback
- avoid heavy infrastructure too early

### Branch B — Real product route
Choose this if LooksMaxxing is strong enough to invest in for actual users.

Next steps:
1. stronger scoring backend (likely CV/ML-backed)
2. improved dual-image battle UX
3. real accounts/data layer
4. monetization plumbing

---

## 5) Minimum Bar Before Showing Someone

Do not present until these are all true:
- app boots cleanly
- upload works
- result feels strong
- tracker looks coherent
- share screen looks postable
- battle mode has both sides populated
- no obviously broken spacing or copy

---

## 6) Current Honest Status

LooksMaxxing is now:
- prototype-complete
- showable
- conceptually strong
- not yet production-grade

That is a good place to be.
