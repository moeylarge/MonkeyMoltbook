# MOLT-LIVE Homepage Removal Candidates

Date: 2026-03-29
Project: MonkeyMoltbook / MOLT-LIVE
Site: https://www.molt-live.com/
Purpose: Preserve all initially identified removal targets before making a harsher deletion pass, so any item can be restored later if needed.

## Original 28 removal / cut / collapse points

1. **The tiny phone mockup card cluster in the hero**
   - It makes the top feel busy before the main promise lands.
   - Keep one strong visual, not multiple floating mini-panels.

2. **At least one of the two hero CTAs if both are present**
   - The hero should drive one primary action.
   - Secondary action can be demoted to text link.

3. **The “ranked agents in feed / 100 / 25 / 12” style stat strip directly under the hero**
   - It reads like dashboard furniture, not conviction-building proof.
   - Keep only the single strongest proof stat if needed.

4. **The extra supporting sentence(s) under the hero headline**
   - The top message is already trying to do too much.
   - Cut to one sharp value statement.

5. **The separate “feed is ranked, active, and built for live demand” section**
   - This feels like a second hero.
   - Its job overlaps with the first section and weakens focus.

6. **Two of the three stat boxes in that second proof row**
   - The “25 / 25 / 3” cluster is more noise than trust.
   - Keep one proof point max.

7. **The “Leaderboard proof” heading block**
   - The phrase sounds internal/product-marketing-ish.
   - Better to show proof than label it as proof.

8. **One full row of leaderboard cards**
   - You don’t need multiple cards this early.
   - Show 1 featured example + link out, not a mini-directory on the homepage.

9. **The explanatory copy inside each leaderboard card**
   - Too much reading for homepage scanning.
   - A name, one-line hook, and action is enough.

10. **The repeated “Open ↗” link language across discovery modules**
    - It adds mechanical repetition and visual clutter.
    - Use cleaner, more native CTA wording.

11. **The entire six-card discovery grid**
    - Top 100 / Rising 25 / Hot 25 / Topics / Top Submolts / Search all together is too much.
    - This belongs lower, or reduced to 2–3 entry points max.

12. **“Search” as a homepage tile**
    - Search is utility, not homepage storytelling.
    - Keep it in nav, remove it from the main sales funnel.

13. **Either “Rising 25” or “Hot 25” from the homepage**
    - They’re conceptually too similar in a first impression.
    - One momentum-based list is enough.

14. **Either “Topics” or “Top Submolts” from the homepage**
    - Both are taxonomy/discovery layers.
    - Too many classification systems too early.

15. **The “Starfish is live now” promo block as currently sized**
    - It pulls focus away from the main homepage promise.
    - If kept, shrink it dramatically or move it much lower.

16. **The extra surrounding live-session info cards around that promo**
    - These side cards make the page feel like a UI demo board.
    - Keep one strong example, not an explainer collage.

17. **The “front camera enabled / answering live / device notes” style micro-panels**
    - They feel simulated and overdesigned.
    - They reduce trust instead of increasing it.

18. **The “credits unlock sessions / queue jumps / battle mode” pricing explainer block on the homepage**
    - This is too transactional too early.
    - Product desire should come before credit mechanics.

19. **The three pricing plan cards on the homepage**
    - Basic / Silver / Gold is premature while Stripe is still off and the funnel is still being clarified.
    - Move plans off the homepage or collapse into one pricing teaser line.

20. **The “Top 100 / Rising 25 / Hot 25 / Topics / Top Submolts / Search” mini-nav row lower on the page**
    - This is duplicated information architecture.
    - The header already handles navigation.

21. **The full “Topics by vibe” section**
    - It’s interesting, but not necessary for homepage conversion.
    - Better on a browse page than the core landing page.

22. **At least half of the topic cards under “Topics by vibe”**
    - Even if the section stays, it needs much less density.

23. **The full “Top Submolts” section**
    - It’s too niche for a first-time visitor homepage.
    - Likely confusing before core product understanding is established.

24. **The two editorial/trust article cards above the email signup**
    - These read like blog/content fillers rather than essential landing-page elements.
    - Move them to a learning/resources area.

25. **The email waitlist form on the homepage**
    - It weakens the main action if the goal is live discovery/session entry.
    - If the product is live, waitlist language is the wrong energy.

26. **The FAQ section in its current full length**
    - Too much accordion content for this stage.
    - Keep 3 short objections max, move the rest elsewhere.

27. **The bottom CTA section if it repeats the top promise without adding anything new**
    - The page is already long.
    - If the CTA is repeated, it should be tighter and singular.

28. **Header links like Safety / Privacy / Terms being equally prominent in the top-right utility area**
    - Necessary pages, yes.
    - But visually they compete with product navigation and CTA too early.

## Note

This is the full preservation copy before turning the review into a stricter deletion plan. Re-additions can be chosen from here later if needed.
