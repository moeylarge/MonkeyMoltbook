## Beat-by-beat editorial intention

## Shot 1 — Central Peak master
**Purpose:** establish world fast

- fade in or hard cut from black
- light cafe ambience can begin here
- if music is used, this is where the tone starts

## Shot 2 — Couples Night sign
**Purpose:** show trigger immediately

- cut cleanly from Shot 1
- keep readable long enough for audience to understand the premise

## Shot 3 — group notices sign
**Purpose:** ensemble problem begins

- first laugh/tension beat starts here
- this is where reactions differentiate the cast

## Shot 4 — Mon + Chance
**Purpose:** chemistry engine #1

- slight emphasis in score or silence can help the tension read
- this beat should feel charged, not romantic-resolution

## Shot 5 — Russ + Rachelle
**Purpose:** chemistry engine #2

- soften the tone slightly
- sweet awkwardness, not broad comedy

## Shot 6 — Jojo + Fufu
**Purpose:** comedy-chaos engine

- bring the sequence back into playful lift
- this beat should make the ensemble feel fun, not just romantic

## Shot 7 — ensemble payoff
**Purpose:** final button

- everyone is now in the weirdness
- end on social instability, warmth, and comedy
- leave slight forward momentum so viewers want the next beat

## Dialogue placement guide

You do not need full dialogue before cutting a first version.

### Minimum viable dialogue structure
- Shot 2: someone reads or notices the sign
- Shot 3: overlapping group reaction
- Shot 4: Chance says something that makes Mon react
- Shot 5: Russ awkwardly reveals himself
- Shot 6: Fufu escalates, Jojo reacts
- Shot 7: ensemble button / final line

### Dialogue priority
If time is low, write only:
1. sign trigger line
2. Chance line
3. Russ awkward line
4. Fufu escalation line
5. final ensemble button
