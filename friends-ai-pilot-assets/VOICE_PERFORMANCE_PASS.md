# NOT A COUPLE — VOICE / PERFORMANCE PASS

## Purpose

This file defines how the current pilot lines should be performed.

It is not just about what each character says.
It is about:
- rhythm
- attitude
- emotional pressure
- social timing
- how each character sounds while saying it

The goal is to preserve ensemble chemistry in performance, not just in writing.

---

## Global performance rules

- keep it warm
- keep it character-driven
- do not overplay punchlines
- let reactions carry weight
- use pauses and eye-contact energy
- avoid sounding like written jokes being recited
- favor sitcom tension over sketch-comedy speed

### Delivery rule
Each line should sound like:
- someone reacting in the moment
- not someone trying to “perform a line”

---

## Character voice anchors

### Mon
- clear
- precise
- controlled
- intelligent
- slightly tense when emotionally exposed
- most of her comedy comes from restraint and irritation

### Chance
- dry
- amused
- smooth but understated
- socially dangerous in a playful way
- enjoys destabilizing people more than he admits

### Russ
- warm
- earnest
- slightly anxious
- thoughtful
- often sounds like he is trying to sound more normal than he actually feels

### Rachelle
- polished
- socially fluent
- observant
- lightly amused when she has the upper hand
- never sloppy, never broad

### Jojo
- open
- warm
- easygoing
- sincere confusion is funny
- never too clever for his own lane

### Fufu
- bright
- expressive
- delighted by chaos
- playful, socially explosive
- should sound like she’s having slightly more fun than everyone else

# LINE-BY-LINE PERFORMANCE NOTES

## SHOT 2
### Line
**Jojo:** “Couples Night?”

### Delivery
- read it like he just noticed something mildly concerning
- not deadpan, not broad
- small confused smile is okay
- should sound like he is inviting everyone else to explain it to him

### Emotional target
- innocent trigger
- soft opening of the conflict

### Rhythm note
Slight upward inflection on “Night?” works.

---

## SHOT 3
### Line
**Fufu:** “No, no — let’s not pretend this isn’t for us.”

### Delivery
- immediate
- delighted
- socially reckless
- she should sound thrilled that the room just became unstable

### Emotional target
- she lights the fuse on purpose

### Rhythm note
Tiny pause after “No, no —” then push the rest with confidence.

---

## SHOT 4A
### Line
**Chance:** “You look tense for someone who’s definitely not part of a couple.”

### Delivery
- dry
- calm
- teasing without raising energy too much
- should sound like he already knows this will get under Mon’s skin

### Emotional target
- soft attack
- playful precision
- flirtation hidden inside provocation

### Rhythm note
Slight emphasis on “definitely” and “couple.”
Do not oversell the sarcasm.

---

## SHOT 4B
### Line
**Mon:** “I’m tense because your face is doing that.”

### Delivery
- clipped
- controlled
- slightly sharper than she means to be
- irritation covering emotional exposure

### Emotional target
- denial through deflection
- she refuses to answer the actual implication

### Rhythm note
Keep it quick.
The speed helps her feel reactive, not rehearsed.

## SHOT 5A
### Line
**Russ:** “Just so we’re all being normal, I’d like to say this sign is weirdly aggressive.”

### Delivery
- earnest
- trying to sound reasonable
- slightly too formal because he’s nervous
- the line is funny because he thinks he’s helping

### Emotional target
- overthinking disguised as group moderation

### Rhythm note
Small breath before “I’d like to say” helps the awkwardness.

# REACTION PRIORITIES BETWEEN LINES

## Most important reaction moments

### After Jojo says “Couples Night?”
- tiny group processing beat

### After Fufu says “this isn’t for us”
- Mon tightens
- Chance enjoys it

### After Chance teases Mon
- Mon reaction matters as much as the line

### After Rachelle exposes Russ
- Russ should visibly fold inward a little

### After Fufu says “one cover-up”
- Jojo looks sincerely lost

### After Chance says he’ll claim Mon
- group reaction is the payoff
- Mon’s face matters most

# PERFORMANCE MISTAKES TO AVOID

## Mon
- don’t make her too harsh
- don’t make her broad or shouty

## Chance
- don’t make him smug in a loud way
- don’t turn him into a Marvel quip machine

## Russ
- don’t make him pathetic
- keep him lovable and earnest

## Rachelle
- don’t make her cruel
- she should feel sharp, not mean

## Jojo
- don’t make him dumb-flat
- he is sweet and sincere, not brainless

## Fufu
- don’t make her random
- her chaos should still feel socially aware

# BEST FIRST-CUT APPROACH

If recording or temp-voicing quickly tonight:
- prioritize tone over perfect polish
- get one usable line read per beat
- preserve spacing between lines
- keep the room feeling alive

The sequence should feel like a real group dynamic, not a joke list.
