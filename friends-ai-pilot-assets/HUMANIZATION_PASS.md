# NOT A COUPLE — HUMANIZATION PASS

## Purpose

This file defines how to make the 64-second pilot version feel as **human** as possible.

Not just polished.
Human.

That means:
- less mechanical joke timing
- more reaction space
- more lived-in overlap
- more room tone
- more eye-contact energy
- more discomfort, hesitation, and tiny shifts in control

The job is not to make the pilot busier.
The job is to make it feel like people are actually in the same room.

---

## Core humanization rule

A scene feels human when:
- not everyone responds instantly
- not every line lands like a punchline
- some people react before they speak
- some people speak to cover what they feel
- silence has meaning
- eye contact changes the temperature

Use those rules more than “make it funnier.”

---

## Global humanization checklist

- leave room before and after the strongest lines
- do not stack all lines back-to-back
- keep cafe ambience present underneath the scene
- let the room breathe after social hits
- let faces do some of the work
- avoid perfect rhythm; slight asymmetry feels more real
- overlap only where it increases realism, not confusion

# WHERE TO PAUSE

## Pause 1 — after “Couples Night?”

### Why
The room needs half a second to realize what this means.

### Effect
This creates the first social vacuum.
It gives Fufu something real to jump into.

---

## Pause 2 — after Fufu’s first escalation

### Why
Mon tightening, Chance enjoying it, Russ collapsing internally — these are funny if visible.

### Effect
The room becomes emotionally readable before the pair scenes begin.

---

## Pause 3 — before Mon answers Chance

### Why
Mon’s face matters more than her words at first.

### Effect
The audience sees that he got under her skin before she denies it.

---

## Pause 4 — before Rachelle’s reply to Russ

### Why
Rachelle should look like she clocks him and then chooses to say something.

### Effect
That makes her feel socially intelligent rather than scripted.

---

## Pause 5 — before Jojo’s “three couples” question

### Why
Jojo is funniest when he sincerely processes the logic.

### Effect
The question feels human instead of punchline-driven.

---

## Pause 6 — after Chance’s final button

### Why
The scene needs room for the hit to spread through the group.

### Effect
The room reaction becomes the ending, not just the line.

---

# WHERE TO OVERLAP LIGHTLY

## Group-processing section
After Fufu lights the fuse, small fragments can overlap softly:
- Mon trying to stop it
- Russ trying to normalize it
- Fufu continuing anyway

Do not make this chaotic wall-of-sound.
Use just enough to feel like a live room.

## Ensemble spiral section
Later in the scene, a small amount of overlapping reaction helps:
- someone objecting under someone else
- Chance amused in the background
- Fufu still pushing

Again: small overlap only.
The audience must still understand the main line.

# HUMAN PERFORMANCE NOTES BY CHARACTER

## Mon
Human version of Mon is not cold.
She is controlled.

### What makes her human
- tiny exhale before a sharp line
- brief delay when emotionally exposed
- visible effort to remain composed

### Avoid
- instant snappiness on every beat
- flat confidence with no vulnerability underneath

---

## Chance
Human version of Chance is calm, not “writerly.”

### What makes him human
- amused restraint
- not rushing the joke
- slight softness under the provocation
- enjoying the room more than performing for it

### Avoid
- smug overdelivery
- line-reading like he knows he has the best dialogue

---

## Russ
Human version of Russ is trying to be normal and failing.

### What makes him human
- verbal over-formality when nervous
- slight breath catches
- visible attempt to self-regulate
- awkward sincerity

### Avoid
- making him pathetic
- making him sound too intentionally funny

---

## Rachelle
Human version of Rachelle is observant before she is witty.

### What makes her human
- she sees the room before she speaks
- her line feels chosen, not automatic
- confidence without performance strain

### Avoid
- turning her into a sarcasm machine
- making her cruel or detached

---

## Jojo
Human version of Jojo is genuine.

### What makes him human
- he really wants to understand what’s happening
- he reacts with warmth, not irony
- he is funny because he means it

### Avoid
- playing him as dumb-flat
- giving him self-aware comedy timing

---

## Fufu
Human version of Fufu is not random-chaotic.
She is socially reckless with delight.

### What makes her human
- she knows exactly what she is stirring up
- she enjoys it visibly
- her escalation has intention

### Avoid
- random “lol chaotic” performance
- yelling the whole time

---

# ROOM TONE / AMBIENCE RULES

To feel human, the scene needs:
- light cafe ambience throughout
- cups / soft room presence if available
- subtle life underneath the scene

It should not feel silent behind the characters.
A room with no air feels fake instantly.

### Keep ambience soft
It should support the humanity, not distract from it.

---

# VISUAL HUMANIZATION

If staying on stills, add human feeling through restraint:
- very light push-ins on emotional beats
- no aggressive zooms
- no flashy transitions
- hold a frame slightly longer than instinct if the reaction matters

### Best candidates for slow push
- Mon + Chance
- Russ + Rachelle
- final ensemble button

---

# WHERE THE SCENE SHOULD FEEL MOST HUMAN

If only three moments get extra care, make it these:

## 1. Mon absorbing Chance’s tease
This is emotional, not just funny.

## 2. Russ getting seen by Rachelle
This is socially intimate and awkward.

## 3. Final room reaction after Chance’s discount line
That is the human payoff of the whole scene.

---

# WHAT "PERFECT" ACTUALLY MEANS HERE

Perfect does **not** mean:
- more lines
- faster jokes
- bigger reactions
- more edits

Perfect means:
- everyone feels like they exist before they speak
- every line shifts the room a little
- the audience feels the discomfort and the affection at the same time
- the comedy comes from relationships, not just wording

That is the standard.
