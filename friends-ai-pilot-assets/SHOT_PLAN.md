# NOT A COUPLE — SHOT PLAN

## Purpose

This file defines the minimum viable visual sequence for the Friends AI pilot:
**Not a Couple**

The goal is not to overbuild.
The goal is to prove:
- cast readability
- environment continuity
- chemistry engines
- episode premise clarity
- comedy/tension rhythm

---

## Pilot premise

At Central Peak, the group notices a **Couples Night** promo.
That simple trigger causes everyone to get weird about who is or is not a couple.

---

## Sequence rule

Build the pilot in the smallest convincing version first.

Target sequence:
1. establish world
2. show trigger
3. show group reaction
4. isolate chemistry engines
5. land ensemble payoff

---

## SHOT 1 — Central Peak establishing shot

### Purpose
Introduce the home-base world.

### Asset
- Central Peak master shot

### What must read
- cozy iconic cafe
- main couch/conversation zone
- counter visible behind
- warm recurring sitcom home-base energy

### Notes
This is the visual “we’re back in the world” shot.

---

## SHOT 2 — Couples Night promo insert

### Purpose
Introduce the episode trigger.

### Asset
- Couples Night promo/sign insert

### What must read
- clearly a romantic/couples promotion
- belongs inside Central Peak
- funny without breaking the world
- enough to trigger social discomfort/comedy

### Notes
This should be visually legible immediately.
If text quality is imperfect, the romantic promo intent still matters most.

---

## SHOT 3 — Group notices the sign

### Purpose
Show all six reacting to the trigger.

### Asset
- full ensemble reaction frame in Central Peak

### What must read
- exactly six characters
- each reaction is distinct
- group energy begins immediately
- sign is the cause of the moment

### Character reaction targets
- **Mon** — controlled, mildly tense
- **Chance** — amused, sly
- **Russ** — awkward, overthinking
- **Rachelle** — poised, intrigued
- **Jojo** — confused but game
- **Fufu** — delighted, ready to escalate chaos

---

## SHOT 4 — Mon + Chance beat

### Purpose
Establish the main banter/chemistry engine.

### Asset
- Mon + Chance pair scene

### What must read
- subtle romantic tension
- charged spacing
- Mon = controlled
- Chance = sly and amused
- not a posed couple photo
- chemistry without explicit confirmation

### Emotional function
This shot tells the audience:
**there is something here, and everyone knows it.**

---

## SHOT 5 — Russ + Rachelle beat

### Purpose
Establish the awkward romantic engine.

### Asset
- Russ + Rachelle pair scene

### What must read
- Russ is nervous and trying his best
- Rachelle is intrigued, not dismissive
- sweet awkward romantic potential
- emotionally readable mismatch in confidence

### Emotional function
This shot tells the audience:
**this dynamic is tender, awkward, and promising.**

---

## SHOT 6 — Jojo + Fufu beat

### Purpose
Establish the comedy-chaos pairing.

### Asset
- Jojo + Fufu pair scene

### What must read
- Jojo is grounding, warm, amused
- Fufu is expressive, stylish, unpredictable
- funny contrast reads instantly
- less romance, more comedic chemistry

### Emotional function
This shot tells the audience:
**these two make scenes fun and unstable.**

---

## SHOT 7 — Ensemble payoff / button

### Purpose
Bring the group energy back together and land the premise.

### Needed asset
- ensemble follow-up frame or reaction beat

### What must read
- the group is now actively weird about couple-status
- someone is escalating the situation
- tone = warm, funny, socially charged

### Notes
This can become the final image or final beat of the short sequence.
If a stronger image emerges later, this shot can evolve.

---

## Optional bridge shots (only if needed)

Use only if the first sequence feels too abrupt.

### Optional A — tighter sign reaction
- closer frame on 2–3 key faces reacting

### Optional B — couch regroup
- group reforming around the Central Peak seating area

### Optional C — line delivery insert
- Chance making it worse
- Mon reacting
- Fufu escalating

Do not add these unless the base sequence needs them.

## Tone rules for the sequence

- warm, not mean
- funny, not chaotic nonsense
- chemistry first
- readability first
- no overcomplicated staging
- every shot should reinforce a character engine

---

## Production stop rule

Do not keep generating random scene assets once the seven core beats work.

Once these are good enough:
- stop expanding
- lock the sequence
- move into pilot assembly / edit planning
