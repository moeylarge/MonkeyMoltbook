# NOT A COUPLE — MOTION PLAN

## Purpose

This file defines the first motion-production phase for the pilot.

The goal is to stop thinking like an animatic and start thinking like a moving scene.

We are **not** trying to animate everything at once.
We are identifying the shots that create the biggest jump in realism and episode feel.

---

## Phase goal

Turn the strongest stills into a first moving version of the sequence.

Priority order:
1. shots that sell world reality
2. shots that sell chemistry
3. shots that sell ensemble life
4. only then support shots

---

## Motion strategy

Do not animate all 7 shots first.
That is wasteful.

Instead:
- animate the **highest-leverage 4 shots first**
- keep the other 3 as stills temporarily
- test whether the sequence already feels much more alive

# SHOT-SPECIFIC MOTION NOTES

## SHOT 01 — Central Peak master

### Goal
Make the world feel alive immediately.

### Desired motion
- subtle camera push-in
- soft ambient cafe life in background if possible
- gentle lighting/life, not chaos

### What should move
- slight camera drift or push
- maybe background life / room energy if the tool supports it

### What should NOT happen
- dramatic camera swing
- bustling chaos
- exaggerated animation

### Runtime target
3–5 seconds

### Why this matters
If the world moves, the whole project starts feeling real.

---

## SHOT 03 — Group notices sign

### Goal
Make the room reaction feel alive.

### Desired motion
- subtle reaction movement
- small head turns / posture shifts / glance changes
- sign remains visually relevant

### What should move
- slight body reactions
- expression shifts
- ensemble noticing the sign

### What should NOT happen
- everyone moving dramatically at once
- broad exaggerated reaction animation
- chaotic scene blocking

### Runtime target
4–6 seconds

### Why this matters
This is the first shot where the audience should believe these six exist together in time, not just in one still frame.

---

## SHOT 04 — Mon + Chance

### Goal
Sell chemistry through motion, not just composition.

### Desired motion
- subtle body tension
- eye contact
- tiny shift in posture
- maybe Chance leaning in slightly / Mon reacting slightly

### What should move
- micro-expression changes
- subtle proximity shift
- maybe a tiny camera push

### What should NOT happen
- overt flirting animation
- sitcom hamminess
- broad gesturing

### Runtime target
5–8 seconds

### Why this matters
This is one of the emotional anchor shots of the pilot.
If this moves well, the whole show feels more alive.

---

## SHOT 07 — ensemble payoff

### Goal
Land the room-wide social chaos with real group life.

### Desired motion
- room reacting as Chance’s line lands
- slight body turns, glances, small physical reactions
- Mon’s reaction should read strongest

### What should move
- subtle ensemble motion
- micro-reactions spreading through the group
- final hold after the line lands

### What should NOT happen
- everyone flailing
- over-animated chaos
- losing clarity of the final beat

### Runtime target
5–8 seconds

### Why this matters
This is the final payoff.
If it moves well, the pilot stops feeling like a slideshow.

# MOTION QUALITY RULES

## Good motion feels like
- subtle
- emotionally legible
- premium
- controlled
- character-driven

## Bad motion feels like
- floaty AI soup
- random head wobble
- rubbery body movement
- overacting
- fake dramatic camera moves

---

# MOTION PRIORITY ORDER

If doing this one-by-one, go in this order:
1. Shot 04 — Mon + Chance
2. Shot 07 — ensemble payoff
3. Shot 03 — group notices sign
4. Shot 01 — Central Peak master

Why:
- chemistry sells the show fastest
- payoff sells the scene
- group shot proves room life
- environment motion is useful but less urgent than character motion

---

# PRACTICAL STOP RULE

Stop Phase 1 once two things are true:
- Mon + Chance motion works
- ensemble payoff motion works

If those two work, the pilot already starts feeling real.

Then decide whether to keep going tonight.
