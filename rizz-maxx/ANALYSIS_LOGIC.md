# ANALYSIS_LOGIC.md

## Framing rule
RIZZ MAXX does not claim objective attractiveness truth. It evaluates dating-profile photo effectiveness and first-impression strength.

## MVP evaluation dimensions
- clarity
- lighting
- facial visibility
- framing
- style presentation
- expressiveness
- perceived confidence
- perceived approachability
- profile variety contribution
- lead-photo strength

## Core outputs
- best lead photo
- strongest overall photos
- weakest photos
- overall profile score
- confidence level
- strengths
- weaknesses
- action plan
- replacement suggestions

## Ranking logic (MVP)
Use weighted heuristic scoring to rank photos across the above dimensions.

### Suggested weighting
- lead-photo strength: 20%
- clarity: 15%
- lighting: 12%
- facial visibility: 12%
- confidence: 12%
- framing: 10%
- style presentation: 8%
- expressiveness: 6%
- approachability: 3%
- profile variety contribution: 2%

## Profile-level logic
Compute overall profile score from:
- average top-photo quality
- lead-photo quality
- weakest-photo drag
- set variety/coherence

## Feedback generation rules
Feedback must be:
- specific
- direct
- practical
- slightly emotionally charged
- never robotic

## Example action outputs
- Replace your current lead photo with the clearest high-confidence image.
- Remove blurry mirror selfies.
- Add one strong full-body shot.
- Use brighter natural light.
- Choose a photo with better eye contact and cleaner framing.

## Premium-only deeper logic
- strongest 6-photo ordering
- deeper per-photo breakdown
- vibe/archetype positioning
- stronger replacement strategy

## Honesty rule
Mark outputs as optimization guidance, not guaranteed dating outcomes.
