# RizzMaxx — Screenshot #7 Crop Spec

## Locked slot
Screenshot #7 for both iPhone and iPad is:

> **Expanded results breakdown / action-plan proof screen**

## Purpose
This screenshot must prove that RizzMaxx is more than a generic photo rater.
It should show useful strategic depth beyond a single score.

---

## Required source state
Use the **premium-unlocked** `ResultsScreen` state.

Reason:
- the free preview proves signal
- the unlocked state proves depth
- screenshot #7 should justify the app’s strategic value, not just repeat screenshot #3

---

## Primary sections to include
Prioritize these sections in order:
1. **What is costing you**
2. **What to do next**
3. **Replacement guidance** (if it fits cleanly)

## Avoid making this the focus
- top hero score block
- best photo block
- first cut block

Those already belong to screenshot #3.

---

## Crop goal
The crop should make the viewer feel:
- this app identifies what is wrong
- this app tells me what to change
- this app gives me a real action plan

---

## iPhone crop recommendation
### Best framing
Center the crop around:
- the start of `What is costing you`
- the full `What to do next` block if possible
- one visible hint of `Replacement guidance` if composition allows

### Visual rule
The screenshot should read as:
- diagnosis
- then action

not as:
- another generic results page

### Keep visible if possible
- section titles
- 2–4 bullets from the negative/problem list
- 2–4 bullets from the next-step/action list

---

## iPad crop recommendation
### Best framing
Use the larger canvas to show:
- `What is costing you`
- `What to do next`
- a cleaner visible portion of `Replacement guidance`

### Why
The tablet version should feel more strategic and product-rich, not just enlarged.

---

## Copy pairing
Use the locked screenshot copy:

### Headline
**Know exactly what to fix next**

### Subheadline
**See what’s working, what’s costing you, and how to strengthen the full set.**

---

## Pass/fail rule
Screenshot #7 passes only if a new viewer can understand, in 2 seconds, that:
- the app explains weaknesses
- the app gives next-step guidance
- the app feels more useful than a one-number score tool

If it reads like a duplicate of screenshot #3, recrop it.
