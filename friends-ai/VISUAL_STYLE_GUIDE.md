# FRIENDS AI
## Visual Style Guide

## Global visual north star
The series should look:
- warm
- inviting
- soft
- upscale but lived-in
- slightly heightened
- stylish without feeling cold
- nostalgic in feeling, modern in finish

Hard rule:
The world should emotionally echo a beloved comfort-sitcom universe, but must not look like a literal recreated set package.

Aesthetic keywords:
- amber
- cream
- walnut
- soft green
- terracotta
- brushed brass
- velvet
- lamp-lit
- conversational
- intimate
- premium cozy

---

## Color language
Global palette:
- warm cream
- muted olive / sage
- caramel / walnut brown
- soft terracotta
- dusty gold / brass
- muted teal accents
- selective jewel tones in wardrobe

Avoid:
- neon overload
- hyper-saturated cartoon color
- sterile white minimalism
- gloomy prestige darkness

---

## Camera / framing rules
Favor:
- medium wides
- two-shots
- ensemble couch framing
- doorway framing
- across-the-hall symmetry
- reaction close-ups used strategically
- warm, stable conversational coverage

Camera feel:
- intimate
- readable
- smooth
- not hyper-kinetic
- not documentary-shaky
- not flashy

---

## Central Peak
Identity:
- social hearth of the series
- public living room
- emotional commons

Core feel:
- half coffee shop
- half living room
- stylish
- warm
- lived-in
- social
- couch-centered
- easy to linger in

Visual priorities:
- iconic couch/seating zone
- main coffee/order counter
- visible espresso/bar area
- warm practical lamps
- layered shelving / wall detail
- at least one window-facing area
- open enough for entrances and reactions

Palette:
- creamy walls
- warm wood
- olive/green accents
- brass/gold details
- soft rust / terracotta seating accents
- chalkboard black for signage

What it should not look like:
- too sleek / clinical
- too rustic-country
- too dim and moody
- too obviously copied from an existing famous café set

## Hallway
Identity:
- mini-stage
- social collision zone

Function:
- doors opening at once
- glances across space
- overheard moments
- near-collisions
- cross-apartment chemistry
- group exits

Requirements:
- clear 2A / 2B logic
- enough width for two- and three-person movement
- warm practical wall lighting
- not sterile
- not dingy

Rule:
The hallway should visually reinforce that these people are always one doorway away from each other.

- repeatability

- richer golden tone at night

---

## Blocking rules
Group scenes should always communicate relationships visually.
Important recurring patterns:
- Mon + Chance ending up near each other
- Russ orienting toward Rachelle
- Jojo + Fufu naturally joining comic space
- Mon and Rachelle owning 2A visually
- Chance and Jojo owning 2B visually
- Russ drifting across apartments

Rule:
The audience should understand emotional dynamics from blocking even with the sound off.

## AI generation consistency rules
Must lock before generation:
- species
- face shape
- eye style
- body proportions
- outfit logic
- palette
- posture
- set layout
- anchor furniture
- key props
- lighting direction

Things that must stay stable episode to episode:
- Central Peak couch zone
- Gunty’s counter area
- 2A layout
- 2B layout
- hallway geometry
- Mon’s elegance
- Chance’s sleekness
- Rachelle’s glamour
- Russ’s awkward softness
- Jojo’s warmth
- Fufu’s expressive energy

Things that can vary:
- outfits within palette logic
- background patrons
- secondary props
- day vs night lighting mood
