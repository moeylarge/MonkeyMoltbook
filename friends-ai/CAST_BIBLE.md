# FRIENDS AI
## Full Cast Bible

## Core cast design rule
Each character must contribute a different kind of energy to the room.

The six emotional weather systems:
- Mon = control
- Russ = sincerity
- Jojo = warmth
- Chance = wit
- Fufu = disruption
- Rachelle = social charge

---

## Mon — Swan
Core identity:
- elegant
- competent
- organized
- sharp
- emotionally invested more deeply than she wants to show

What she brings:
- structure
- standards
- pressure
- intelligence
- romantic denial
- beautiful irritation

Speech pattern:
- clear
- precise
- economical
- strong sentence endings
- slightly elevated wording when irritated

Line qualities:
- concise
- exact
- corrective
- dry when defensive
- controlled when upset

Examples:
- “That is not what happened.”
- “You’re making this worse.”
- “I’m not doing this with you.”
- “That is a ridiculous sentence.”

Scene behavior:
- resists the premise first
- raises the standard of the room
- becomes revealing through denial
- reacts best to Chance and Fufu

Should never become:
- just mean
- humorless
- robotic
- joyless
- a flat cold perfectionist copy

---

## Russ — Giraffe
Core identity:
- thoughtful
- sincere
- awkward
- romantic
- earnest
- too mentally active for his own peace

What he brings:
- heart
- longing
- overthinking
- vulnerability
- accidental comedy

Speech pattern:
- longer thoughts
- self-corrections
- qualifiers
- logic bridges
- thinking while talking

Line qualities:
- thoughtful
- overexplained
- earnest
- vulnerable
- gently spiraling

Examples:
- “I know, but that’s not exactly what I meant.”
- “If you look at the sequence of events—”
- “I’m not panicking. I’m trying to understand the structure.”

Scene behavior:
- reacts sincerely where others posture
- turns subtext into too much text
- needs Jojo or Chance to bounce off

Should never become:
- pathetic
- creepy
- whiny
- spineless
- bitter

---

## Jojo — Golden Retriever
Core identity:
- warm
- charming
- easygoing
- open-hearted
- socially fearless
- instinctively affectionate

What he brings:
- warmth
- momentum
- social ease
- accidental chaos
- optimism
- emotional cushioning

Speech pattern:
- casual
- fast when excited
- direct
- emotionally certain even with no evidence
- weirdly memorable phrasing

Line qualities:
- easy
- funny
- surprising
- unfiltered
- charmingly dumb but not empty

Examples:
- “No, that’s definitely something.”
- “You’re both acting very couple-shaped right now.”
- “I’m not wrong, I’m early.”

Scene behavior:
- keeps scenes from going cold
- throws gasoline on situations with a smile
- validates feelings no one wants validated

Should never become:
- irritatingly dumb
- fake-happy
- mean
- empty comic relief

## Chance — Fox
Core identity:
- witty
- observant
- dry
- emotionally evasive
- attractive
- socially fluid

What he brings:
- banter
- romantic charge
- social intelligence
- undercutting wit
- teasing pressure

Speech pattern:
- brief
- dry
- clean wording
- understated
- quiet precision
- rarely obvious emotional language

Line qualities:
- smooth
- lean
- teasing
- sharp
- deceptively simple

Examples:
- “That seems unlikely.”
- “You’re reacting a lot for someone who doesn’t care.”

Scene behavior:

- lands the cleanest line
- makes subtext visible

Should never become:

- cruel
- too detached to care
- overtalkative

Speech pattern:

- intuitive leaps

Core identity:
- glamorous
- socially aware
- polished
- romantic
- emotionally perceptive
- vulnerable under polish

What she brings:
- social charge
- beauty
- flirtation
- romantic momentum
- style
- emotional sensitivity

Speech pattern:

- lightly teasing
- socially precise
- confident language hiding uncertainty

Line qualities:

- slightly performative

## Gunty
Core identity:
- observant
- dry
- grounded
- familiar
- recurring café constant

What he brings:
- continuity
- rhythm
- deadpan punctuation
- café realism
- outside perspective on the group

Speech pattern:
- minimal
- dry
- clear
- low emotional display
- devastating simplicity

Line qualities:
- short
- deadpan
- efficient
- observational

Examples:
- “That was not support.”
- “You physically formed them.”
- “So... six full-price drinks.”

Scene behavior:
- enters late with the best line
- confirms what the audience already sees
- makes Central Peak feel lived-in

Should never become:
- broad
- goofy
- overtalkative
- scene-stealing all the time

---

## Group dynamic rule
When tension enters:
- Mon controls it
- Russ overthinks it
- Jojo normalizes it
- Chance teases it
- Fufu exposes it
- Rachelle stylizes it
- Gunty deadpans it

## Group warmth rule
Even when they needle, expose, and overreact, the audience must still feel: these people belong together.
