# FRIENDS AI — MASTER BIBLE

## Working title
Friends AI

## Core premise
An original comfort sitcom about six close friends orbiting a cozy café and two across-the-hall apartments, built to give audiences the warmth, chemistry, and emotional homecoming of a beloved friend-group classic — without copying protected names, voices, music, or story identity.

---

## NORTH STAR

### Mission
Create an original ensemble sitcom that feels like coming home.

The audience should feel:
- warmth
- familiarity
- comfort
- longing
- chemistry
- friendship as home

The show should deliver:
- repeatable hangout energy
- lovable group dynamics
- soft romantic tension
- small problems that become socially huge
- a world people want to revisit

### Core sentence
Make people feel like they got the reunion feeling they emotionally wanted, without ever pretending they literally got one.

---

## FORMAT

### Current launch format
- Pilot: 4 minutes
- Goal: prove chemistry, tone, world, and repeatability

### Long-term potential
- 4-minute episodes
- 8–12 minute mini-episodes
- eventual long-form special or sitcom-format expansion if the world proves itself

### Success condition
The audience should finish the pilot wanting:
- episode 2
- more time in the world
- more scenes with the cast
- more relationship progression

---

## TONE

### Tone words
- warm
- cozy
- witty
- charming
- emotionally safe
- stylish
- conversational
- playful
- slightly heightened
- affectionate

### Tone rule
Affection must always be stronger than sarcasm.

### The show is not
- cynical
- edgy for the sake of edge
- mean
- lore-heavy
- action-driven
- dark prestige TV
- random internet sketch chaos

## COMFORT ANCHORS

Repeatable anchors:
- Central Peak café
- apartment 2A
- apartment 2B
- the hallway between them
- the same six-character social orbit
- Gunty as recurring café anchor
- recurring pair tensions
- familiar rituals and entrances/exits

Comfort formula:
- predictable environment
- unpredictable interaction

## WORLD

### Central Peak
The core café/lounge where the group naturally gathers every day.

Function:
- gossip center
- flirtation zone
- public arena
- conflict ignition point
- group reset point
- recurring visual home base

Feel:
- half coffee shop
- half living room
- slightly stylish
- very hangout-able
- couch-friendly
- conversation-first
- warm and iconic

### Apartment 2A
Residents:
- Mon
- Rachelle

Function:
- polished emotional hub

Feel:
- beautiful
- curated
- aesthetically strong
- stylish
- slightly aspirational
- lived-in but controlled

### Apartment 2B
Residents:
- Chance
- Jojo

Function:
- relaxed comedic hub

Feel:
- less polished
- more casual
- more improvised
- joke-friendly
- lived-in
- slightly messy but charming

### Hallway
Function:
- overheard moments
- interruption comedy
- near-collisions
- awkward timing
- doorframe flirting
- emotional spillover between 2A and 2B

Rule:
Because 2A and 2B face each other across the hall, the whole show gains mobility, intimacy, visual rhythm, and classic sitcom flow.

## RELATIONSHIP MAP

### Primary engines
- Mon + Chance = strongest banter pairing; control vs slipperiness; irritation with chemistry underneath
- Russ + Rachelle = awkward romantic engine; sincerity vs glamour; sweet and emotionally durable
- Jojo + Fufu = comedy accelerator; enthusiasm + chaos; often funniest side pairing

### Secondary engines
- Mon + Rachelle = stylish friendship; subtle comparison; support mixed with tension
- Chance + Russ = dry wit vs earnest spiral
- Jojo + Russ = sweetness; comfort; emotional safety
- Fufu + Mon = order vs disruption
- Fufu + Rachelle = glam vs absurd

---

## EPISODE ENGINE

Best stories are small social problems that become emotionally huge.

Episode fuel:
- who likes whom
- who is pretending not to care
- who gave bad advice
- who feels excluded
- who is trying to control the group
- who is jealous
- who is being performative
- who is overreading a tiny signal
- who is accidentally acting like a couple
- who is emotionally spiraling over nothing

Rule:
The stories should feel familiar, social, character-driven, low-stakes externally, and high-stakes internally.

---

## ORIGINALITY RULES

This show must stay original in:
- names
- voices
- music
- dialogue rhythm
- visual identity
- scene construction
- branding
- episode beats
- emotional phrasing

We can preserve:
- warmth
- ensemble comfort
- chosen family
- café/apartment rhythm
- romantic group tension
- cozy repeatability

We must avoid:
- surface imitation
- soundalike voices
- obvious character clones
- near-copy intro energy
- direct reference-dependent writing
- scenes that feel like recreated Friends moments

Hard rule:
Translate function, not surface.

### Length
4 minutes

### Premise

### Why it works

- the café
- the cast
- the social tone
- the romantic map
- the humor style
- the apartment/hallway engine

It proves:
- chemistry
- repeatability
- ensemble readability
- warmth under comedy
